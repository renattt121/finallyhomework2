Air Jordan Collection

Air Jordan 1

Released in 1985 — the start of sneaker culture. First signature model of Michael Jordan with Nike.

Air Jordan 2

1986 — premium Italian materials, elegant design. Less known but highly collectible.

Air Jordan 3

1988 — designed by Tinker Hatfield with iconic elephant print and visible Air unit.

Air Jordan 4

1989 — iconic silhouette with mesh panels and signature Air cushioning.

Air Jordan 5

1990 — reflective tongue, lace locks, inspired by WWII fighter planes.

Air Jordan 6

1991 — Michael Jordan won his first NBA championship wearing these.

Air Jordan 7

1992 — Olympic Dream Team style, inspired by African tribal patterns.

Air Jordan 8

1993 — bold straps, flashy design, strong support for ankles.

Air Jordan 9

1994 — inspired by MJ’s global reach; visible on international courts.

Air Jordan 10

1994 — career achievements engraved on the sole.

Air Jordan 11

1995 — patent leather, iconic on-court and off-court style.

Air Jordan 12

1996 — elegant, durable, power on court, used in NBA finals.

Jordan History & Legacy

1985 - The Beginning

The Air Jordan line started with Jordan 1, revolutionizing basketball shoes and streetwear culture.

1991 - First Championship

Jordan won his first NBA title wearing Air Jordan 6, cementing the shoes in history.

Global Influence

From the court to fashion streets worldwide, Air Jordans became a symbol of culture.

About Air Jordan

Air Jordan is more than sneakers — it's a cultural icon. Designed for Michael Jordan, each model combines innovation, style, and history. The line influenced sports, street fashion, and global culture for decades.
